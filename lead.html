01 THE 24-HOUR BATTLE PLAN — HOUR BY HOUR
⚔️
Your Exact 24-Hour Closing Schedule
Follow this to the minute. This sequence is optimized for maximum conversions.
🚨
CRITICAL: Most salespeople fail because they have no system. They call randomly, get rejected, and quit. This schedule prevents that. Follow each block exactly — it is designed around Indian business owners' habits, lunch breaks, and decision-making windows.
1
Hour 1–2: 7:00AM–9:00AM — INTELLIGENCE & TARGETING (Never skip this)
Pull your lead list before calling. Use Google Maps, JustDial, IndiaMart, or walk your target area. For each lead, note: (1) Business name, (2) Owner's name if possible, (3) Do they have a website? (4) Is their GMB listing complete? (5) Any recent reviews? This 2-hour prep work will make every call 5x more powerful because you open with a SPECIFIC observation, not a generic pitch.
2
Hour 3–5: 9:00AM–11:00AM — FIRST CALL BLOCK (Prime Time for North India)
Most shop and clinic owners are at their business by 9:30AM and not yet overwhelmed. This is your gold window. Target: 15–20 calls. Accept that 12–14 will not connect or will say call back. That's normal. You need 5–6 real conversations to get 1–2 demo appointments. Do not stop at 5 calls. Volume is everything in the first block.
3
Hour 6: 11:00AM–12:00PM — WhatsApp Blast (While Calls Were Going)
While making calls, simultaneously send WhatsApp messages to 30–40 businesses you found but couldn't reach by phone. Use voice notes for manufacturing/industrial leads — they convert 3x better than text. Text messages for coaches and consultants. Include your portfolio image or before/after screenshot in EVERY WhatsApp message.
4
Hour 7: 12:00–1:00PM — LUNCH BREAK + REVIEW (Sacred rest, but plan next block)
Don't call during this window — business owners are at lunch or with family. Use this hour to: (1) Review which calls gave the most engagement, (2) Look up the specific businesses of callbacks, (3) Prepare your demo materials — open your best portfolio examples, (4) Draft personalized WhatsApp messages for the warm leads from your morning calls.
5
Hours 8–10: 2:00PM–5:00PM — DEMO BLOCK + SECOND CALL WAVE
If you secured morning demo appointments, do them NOW. Video demos via Zoom/Google Meet — share your screen showing their current situation vs your solution. Simultaneously keep calling South India and West India targets (they are 1–2hrs ahead on decision cycles). Target 15 more calls. By 5PM you should have given 2–4 demos.
6
Hour 11: 5:00PM–6:30PM — THE GOLDEN FOLLOW-UP WINDOW
This is statistically the highest-conversion window for Indian SMEs. Owners are winding down their day, not yet at home. They have 15 minutes to talk. Call every person who said "baad mein batata hoon" or "sochta hoon." This is when you convert maybes to yeses. Have your booking link and payment UPI QR ready.
7
Hour 12: 6:30PM–8:00PM — CLOSE CALL + ADVANCE PAYMENT
For your warmest lead from the day — the one who said "interesting, tell me more" — call them now with a time-limited offer. Example: "Sir, today only I can offer ₹2,000 off if we lock in this evening. After today, that slot goes to another client in your area. Can we do 50% advance to book?" Take UPI payment immediately — don't let them sleep on it.
8
Hour 13–16: Evening + Next Morning — NURTURE FOLLOW-UPS
Send a post-conversation WhatsApp thank-you message to EVERY person you spoke with, including those who said no. Many people who initially say no buy within 7 days when they see consistent, professional follow-up. "Sir, thanks for your time today — just sent a sample to your WhatsApp. No pressure at all, just wanted you to have it."

Website Design/Redesign Targets
Find businesses with existing bad websites — the audit approach
FREE AUDIT TOOLS — USE THESE BEFORE CALLING
1
Google PageSpeed Insights — pagespeed.web.dev
Enter any website URL → Get score out of 100. Score below 50 = urgent lead. Below 30 = emergency lead. Screenshot the score — send it as your opening message. This isn't opinion, it's Google's own data. No owner can argue with Google.
2
Mobile Screenshot Test
Open their website on your phone → Screenshot it → See how it actually looks. If text is tiny, layout is broken, or buttons overlap → DESIGN LEAD. Send them that actual screenshot with a circle/arrow drawn over the problem. Visual evidence is 10x more powerful than words.
3
GTmetrix — gtmetrix.com
Run the speed test → You get load time, page size, and a detailed report. A load time over 4 seconds = strong pitch. "Sir, your website is taking 7 seconds to load. Google ranks fast sites higher. We can cut that to under 2 seconds."
WHERE TO FIND REDESIGN LEADS (FAST)
  • IndiaMart supplier pages: Search any category → Click "company profile" → Visit their website link. 70% will be outdated/broken. Note the name and send a WhatsApp audit.
  • Google search pages 3–6: Search "[industry] in [city]" — the businesses on page 3+ are there because their site is technically weak. Those are your leads.
  • TradeIndia.com directory: Filter by city + industry → Check websites → Those with 2010-era designs are perfect targets.
  • LinkedIn company profiles: Filter by industry + city → Check their website link → Old or broken = design lead.
  • Zomato/Practo profile links: Click the website link on any listed business → Many will be broken or ancient.
  • Chamber of Commerce member lists: Most city Chambers publish member directories online — vast majority have outdated sites.

HOW TO DEMO — EXACT SEQUENCE
1
Ask to see their current method (1 min)
"Abhi billing kaise karte hain? Kuch dikhao na — register/Excel/phone?" Let them show you. This makes them feel heard AND reveals their pain points. Never skip this step.
2
Open your demo on your laptop (immediately)
Don't talk about features. Type in a product name → add price → generate bill. Show it printed in 10 seconds. The visual impact is instant. Their eyes will widen. Say nothing. Let the demo speak.
3
"Aapke shop ka ek item ka naam batao" (Ask for their product)
Now enter THEIR actual product/customer name into your demo. Make it personal to their business. When they see their own business name on a professional GST invoice — psychological ownership kicks in. They start imagining using it.
4
Show the customer history feature
"Ab agar aap customer ka naam search karein — uski saari previous purchases aa jayengi." This single feature alone closes 40% of deals. They IMMEDIATELY think of 3–4 customers who owe them money or whose history they've lost.
5
Show GST report
"Mahine ke end mein ek button press karo — CA ko ye file bhej do. ITR filing easy." This closes accountant-referred leads instantly.

How to go from demo → price → advance payment in one call
DEMO STRUCTURE (WORKS FOR ALL SERVICES)
1
Start: Ask, don't tell (2 min)
Before showing anything: "Sir, pehle batao — abhi sabse badi problem kya hai? Kyun aap yahan tak aaye/baat kar rahe ho?" Let them speak for 60–90 seconds. Take mental notes. THIS IS YOUR AMMO for the close. Use their exact words when describing your solution.
2
Show the current situation (1 min)
Show THEIR current Google listing or website on YOUR screen. "Yeh aapki current situation hai online — [describe what you see]." This creates contrast. They SEE the problem with their own eyes before you show the solution.
3
Demo the solution (5–7 min)
Show portfolio or live demo. Keep commentary minimal — let them react. Ask "Yeh jaisa chahte the?" or "Does this match what you were looking for?" Questions engage them; monologue puts them to sleep.
4
Connect solution to their problem (1 min)
Use their exact words from step 1: "Aapne bataya tha ki [their exact words] — yeh exactly woh solve karta hai by [mechanism]." This shows you listened and that your solution is personalized, not generic.
5
The pricing conversation (2 min)
Never just say a number. Frame it first: "Investment ke baare mein — yeh ek time cost hai, ek saal mein recover ho jaati hai for most businesses. Specifically aapke liye [₹X]. Iske saath [list what's included]." Then say the number confidently and STOP TALKING.
6
The close question (critical)
Never: "Kya aap lena chahte hain?" (weak, invites no). Instead: "Toh kab start karein — aaj advance le lun aur kal se kaam shuru?" OR "Aapko 50% advance do karni hoti hai start karne ke liye — UPI bheju?" Give them ACTION, not a decision between yes/no.
